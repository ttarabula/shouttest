Methodology & caveats

Name frequencies come from the City of Toronto's Licensed Dog and Cat Names dataset (2023 — the most recent complete year). The dataset only publishes the top 200 names; anything off-chart is invisible to us. A dog's "name share" is its count divided by the 18,467 registered dogs in that year's top-200.

Park occupancy is an unscientific piecewise estimate based on area_sqm from the Off-Leash Areas dataset. A 128-sqm postage-stamp gets ~3 dogs; High Park gets ~40. Real attendance varies by time, weather, and whether the guy with the tennis-ball chucker is there.

Given n dogs and name-share p, expected heads turned is n × p and the probability of at least one reaction is 1 − (1 − p)n. We assume independence, which is wrong (dogs chain-react), but by the time we fix that you're already home.

Methodology

The patron name is a deterministic hash of the park's name into the top 100 dog names — pick the same park, get the same patron. Every other line in the reading is the park's actual amenity record from the Off-Leash Areas dataset, with the stated meanings assigned by this office. Today's energy rotates daily.
